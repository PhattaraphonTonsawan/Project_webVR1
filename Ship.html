The USS Missouri (BB-63)

The USS Missouri (BB-63), nicknamed "Mighty Mo," is a legendary Iowa-class battleship and one of the most iconic warships in U.S. naval history. Commissioned in 1944, it served during World War II, the Korean War, and the Gulf War. Armed with nine 16-inch (406 mm) guns and numerous smaller weapons, it combined immense firepower with a top speed of 33 knots. The Missouri is most famous as the site of Japan's surrender on September 2, 1945, ending World War II. Decommissioned in 1992, it now serves as a museum ship at Pearl Harbor, symbolizing U.S. military strength and history.
