The B-17 Flying Fortress

The B-17 Flying Fortress was a durable and heavily armed bomber used extensively by the U.S. Army Air Forces during World War II. Known for its resilience and accuracy, it featured four Wright Cyclone engines, a range of 2,000 miles, and up to 13 .50 caliber machine guns for defense. Capable of carrying 4,800 lbs of bombs, it played a pivotal role in strategic daylight bombing campaigns over Europe, including the Schweinfurt-Regensburg missions and support for D-Day. Despite high early losses, the B-17 became a symbol of Allied air power, with a legacy preserved in museums and airshows today.
